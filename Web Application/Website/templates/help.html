Help Page: Q&A

    How is the Pairwise comparison working:

  • In the Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP), pairwise comparison involves comparing elements of a
    decision-making hierarchy to determine their relative importance or preference. Users assign numerical
    values to indicate the strength of preference or importance between pairs of elements. This process helps
    to quantify subjective judgments and preferences.
    User input is crucial in AHP because it reflects their preferences, values, and priorities, which are
    essential for making informed decisions. By involving users in pairwise comparisons, AHP captures their
    subjective assessments, leading to more accurate and meaningful results. This input ensures that the
    final decision reflects the stakeholders' preferences and goals.


  • Why do I need to use the Saaty's scale?

  • The Saaty's scale is a set of numerical values used in the Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP), a
    decision-making methodology. It provides a consistent and structured way for decision-makers to
    express their preferences in pairwise comparisons. The scale is essential for creating a matrix
    of comparisons, which is then used in mathematical calculations to determine priority weights
    for decision criteria. The Saaty's scale ensures that judgments are consistent, allowing for a
    reliable and systematic decision-making process.


    • More information about the calculation:

  • In AHP, after users provide pairwise comparisons, the calculations involve three main steps:
    1) Normalization: Pairwise comparison matrices are normalized to ensure consistency
    and comparability. Each comparison matrix is transformed into a matrix of priority values by dividing
    each element by the sum of its column.
    2) Weight Calculation: The priority values obtained from normalization are averaged across
    each row to calculate the weight of each criterion or alternative. These weights represent the relative
    importance of each element in the hierarchy.
    3) Consistency Check: A consistency check is performed to ensure the reliability of the comparisons
    made by users. The Consistency Ratio (CR) is calculated to assess the consistency of the judgments
    provided. If the CR exceeds a certain threshold (typically 0.1), users may need to review and adjust
    their comparisons to improve consistency.
